# **📄 Case Study: Phishing Attack on College Students**





###### Phishing is one of the most common forms of cybercrime, where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data. A realistic scenario involves a group of college students receiving an email that appears to come from their university’s IT department. The email warns them that their student portal account will be locked unless they immediately verify their login credentials through a provided link.

###### The link directs them to a fake website designed to look identical to the official portal. Unaware of the fraud, several students enter their usernames and passwords. Within hours, attackers gain access to their accounts, steal personal information, and even send further phishing emails to their contacts.

###### Typically, phishing targets individuals who are less experienced with online security, such as first-year students. The consequences can be severe: compromised academic records, unauthorized access to financial accounts, and reputational damage if attackers misuse the stolen data.

###### This case highlights how phishing exploits trust and urgency. By disguising malicious intent under the appearance of authority, attackers manipulate victims into acting quickly without verifying authenticity.
